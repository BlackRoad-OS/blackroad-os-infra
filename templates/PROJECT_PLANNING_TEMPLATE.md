## ğŸ’¡ Best Practices

### Writing Good OKRs

**Objectives should:**
- Inspire and motivate the team
- Be qualitative and directional
- Answer "What do we want to achieve?"
- Examples: "Become the fastest deployment platform", "Deliver exceptional developer experience"

**Key Results should:**
- Be quantitative and measurable
- Answer "How will we know we achieved it?"
- Use numbers, percentages, or binary outcomes
- Examples: "Reduce deployment time from 10min to 2min", "Achieve 95% test coverage", "Launch 3 new integrations"

### Progress Tracking

- **Update regularly**: Weekly or bi-weekly updates keep everyone aligned
- **Be honest**: ğŸ”´ and ğŸŸ¡ indicators help surface issues early
- **Celebrate wins**: Use ğŸŸ¢ and emojis to recognize progress
- **Review together**: Use in team meetings for visibility

### Common Pitfalls

âŒ **Don't:**
- Set too many objectives (3 is ideal)
- Make KRs too vague ("improve performance")
- Forget to update progress
- Hide blockers or risks

âœ… **Do:**
- Keep objectives ambitious but achievable
- Make KRs specific and time-bound
- Update status weekly
- Surface blockers immediately
- Celebrate completed KRs
